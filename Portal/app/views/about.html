"We get fibre built to you"

Whats going on?

The demand for fast, reliable fixed broadband in South Africa has skyrocketed! A quick look around indicates that demand exceeds supply…..BUT the problem is that fibre infrastructure companies will only build fibre to your home when sufficient demand is created in your area – it has to make business sense for them to build.

How does it work?

The way that fibre to the home/business (FTTx) works is simple. There are companies that build open access fibre to your area, and there are companies (called ISP’s) that provide services (e.g. broadband internet, video, voice) over this infrastructure to you. Some do both. The majority of fibre infrastructure providers are open access – which means that any ISP that has an agreement with them can provide you with super fast access.

This is great for places where infrastructure is built or is going to be built soon, but why does it feel like these infrastructure providers are not listening to you? Why are they not building fibre to you even though you have shown demand? Are you not important enough? Are you not living in an area “deserving” enough? Are there not enough people in your area that are showing demand (even though all of your neighbours say they want it)?

The answer is simple….DILUTION.

What is the problem?

Simply put, there are far too many places to indicate your interest – practically all ISP’s and network providers give you the ability to do this. However, for the true demand to be shown to any one of these providers they have to have the FULL picture. Lets take an example:
  • Assume that the business case for any fibre infrastructure provider requires that demand in an area needs to be at least 30%.
  • If we take a hypothetical area that has 100 people, that means that 30 people need to be interested in fibre for it to be built.
  • Assume that these 30 people show their interest to fibre infrastructure providers via ISP’s or directly to the infrastructure providers.
  • Now lets do the maths – if each of the 30 people go to the 10 (its probably more) different places to log interest, then your neighbouhood will only have an interest shown of 3 per infrastructure provider.
  • In other words, while the demand is 30%, it is only shown as 3%!
Now apply the same logic to a similar size area where all 100 people want fibre – the dilution factor (in the worst case) results in 10% interest only. Yet, it is almost guaranteed that any provider that gets 100% interest will build to your area immediately!

Can you see the problem?
